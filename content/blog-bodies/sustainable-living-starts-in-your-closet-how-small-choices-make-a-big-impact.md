Sustainability starts at home — and one of the most overlooked places to make a difference is your closet. The average person cycles through clothing faster than ever before, but with mindful care and eco-friendly practices you can dramatically reduce waste and extend the life of your wardrobe.

### **The Hidden Environmental Cost of Fast Fashion**

*   The fashion industry produces **10% of global carbon emissions**, and textile waste fills landfills faster than ever.
*   Many clothes are discarded long before their usable life ends — often because they’re damaged, faded, or improperly cared for.
*   Traditional laundry and dry cleaning methods contribute to **water contamination and chemical waste** that affect local communities.

### **How Everyday Habits Can Create Change**

You don’t need to overhaul your entire lifestyle to make a difference. Small shifts in how you clean and store your clothes can have a lasting impact:

*   **Air out garments** after wearing instead of washing every time.
*   **Use eco-friendly dry cleaning** to avoid toxic chemicals and preserve fabrics.
*   **Mend, tailor, or repurpose** garments instead of discarding them.
*   **Donate responsibly** to charities or textile recyclers.

### **The Regional Ripple Effect**

*   In **South Florida**, eco-conscious cleaning helps protect beaches, coral reefs, and coastal ecosystems.
*   In **New York and Connecticut**, sustainable habits reduce chemical pollution in densely populated areas.
