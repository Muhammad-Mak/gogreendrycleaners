## **Why Traditional Petrochemical Dry Cleaning Can Harm Delicate Fabrics**

For decades, the dry-cleaning industry relied heavily on petrochemical-based solvents, most notably perchloroethylene(commonly called “perc”). While perc cleans oils and stains effectively, it also creates several problems for delicate garments. The solvent is aggressive and interacts with natural fibers—such as silk and wool—by swelling them, disrupting their shape, or causing dye lift.

Many customers who have experienced fading, thinning, lingering chemical odor, stiffness, or shrinkage often discover that these issues stem not from wear-and-tear, but from harsh traditional solvents.

Traditional petrochemical solvents can weaken ornamentation, adhesives, interlinings, sequins, and metallic fibers found in evening gowns or couture garments. Even wool, which is naturally durable, can react poorly when subjected to high-solvent loads that strip natural lanolin oils, leaving the fiber dry and brittle.

## **How Petrochemical-Free Dry Cleaning Works (and Why It’s Safer)**

Petrochemical-free systems use**biodegradable, low-impact, non-toxic solvents**designed to clean effectively without disrupting fabric structure. While different green dry-cleaners use slightly different solvent blends, the overall process remains similar:

1.   **Solvents are plant-derived or CO₂-based**, ensuring they break down safely without leaving irritating residues.
2.   **The cleaning environment is temperature-controlled**, protecting proteins found in silk, wool, and cashmere.
3.   **Agitation is reduced**, preventing stretching, pilling, friction damage, or bead loosening.
4.   **Moisture levels are precisely regulated**, preventing shrinking while still allowing deep cleaning.

The entire process is designed around fiber preservation. Unlike traditional solvents, these alternatives interact gently with proteins and natural oils, keeping delicate fabrics supple, vibrant, and structurally sound.

## **The Fabric Science Behind Petrochemical-Free Solvents**

The success of petrochemical-free cleaning is rooted in chemistry. These solvents work on a molecular level, dissolving oils and soils without altering the molecular bonds of delicate fabrics.

### **No Fiber Swelling**

Solvents like perc penetrate fibers deeply, swelling them and displacing structural bonds. Petrochemical-free solvents remain on the surface, dissolving contaminants without disturbing the core fiber structure.

### **Controlled Polarity**

Many traditional solvents have polarity levels that strip dyes. Petrochemical-free solvents are engineered to clean oils without lifting or smudging color molecules. This allows garments to maintain original shades longer, with minimal fading.

### **Gentle Temperature Range**

Heat intensifies chemical reactions. Green solvents operate at lower and more consistent temperatures, reducing the risk of:

*   shrinkage
*   glue softening
*   texture change
*   fiber weakening

This is especially important for animal-based fibers like wool, silk, cashmere, and angora.

### **Moisture Balance**

Some delicate fabrics respond poorly to full immersion in water, but they still benefit from controlled amounts of moisture. Petrochemical-free systems use micro-moisture technology that loosens stains while avoiding full saturation, keeping fibers safe.
